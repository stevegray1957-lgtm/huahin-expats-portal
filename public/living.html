🏘️ Hua Hin Neighbourhoods

Hua Hin stretches about 20km along the Gulf coast. The right neighbourhood depends on your lifestyle — whether you want beach access, expat community, peace and quiet, or city convenience.

Central / Old Town

City Centre (Soi 88–Soi 112)

The hub of expat activity. Walking distance to Night Market, restaurants, banks and the Weekend Market. Higher traffic but maximum convenience. Great for first-time expats.

Condo: ฿8,000–฿20,000/mo | House: ฿18,000–฿40,000/mo

Beach Area

Hua Hin Beach (Soi 1–Soi 50)

Premium location near the main beach and Cicada Night Market. Many luxury condos and serviced apartments. Touristy but well-connected. Best for shorter stays or beach lovers.

Condo: ฿12,000–฿35,000/mo | House: ฿30,000–฿80,000/mo

Popular with Expats

Khao Takiab / South Hua Hin

Quieter beach area 5km south of town. Popular with European expats. More affordable than the centre with a relaxed vibe. Excellent seafood restaurants along the beachfront.

Condo: ฿7,000–฿16,000/mo | House: ฿15,000–฿35,000/mo

Quiet & Affordable

Nong Kae / Pranburi Road

Further south, quieter and very affordable. Popular with long-term expats who prefer space over convenience. Some beautiful lagoon-view properties. 15 min drive to town.

Condo: ฿5,000–฿12,000/mo | House: ฿12,000–฿25,000/mo

Local Feel

Hua Hin Hills / Golf Areas

Inland areas near the golf courses. Popular with golfers. Beautiful hillside views, large houses with gardens. Requires a car. Very quiet, local-style living.

House: ฿15,000–฿45,000/mo | Villa with pool: ฿35,000–฿80,000/mo

Growing Area

Cha-Am (30km North)

Technically a different town but popular with Hua Hin expats for its even quieter beaches and lower prices. Good Thai and European restaurants. Easy drive to Hua Hin.

Condo: ฿5,000–฿10,000/mo | House: ฿10,000–฿20,000/mo

💡 Tip: Most expats rent for 6–12 months before deciding where to buy or settle long-term. Use the Real Estate Directory to find reputable local agents who know the market.

🌤️ Hua Hin Climate

Hua Hin sits on the west coast of the Gulf of Thailand and has one of the most pleasant climates in Thailand — less rain than Phuket or Koh Samui, warm year-round, and good sea breezes.

Month Avg Temp Rain Days Conditions
Jan–Feb24–30°C2–4🌞 Dry season — ideal weather
Mar–Apr27–33°C4–7☀️ Hot, still dry — good beach weather
May–Jun28–34°C8–12🌦️ Light rain starts
Jul–Aug27–32°C12–16🌧️ Rainy season — still warm
Sep–Oct26–31°C15–20⛈️ Wettest months — brief storms
Nov–Dec24–30°C5–10🌞 Dry season returns

Good to know: Even during the "rainy season", rain typically falls in short bursts and the sun returns quickly. Hua Hin gets far less rain than Phuket or Koh Samui. Many expats consider October–November the best value time to be here.

🛒 Shopping in Hua Hin

Hua Hin has excellent shopping options for expats — from large supermarkets to specialist imported food stores.

🛍️ Makro (Cash & Carry)

Large warehouse store with excellent range of imported and local food. Popular with expats for bulk buying. Good wine and spirits selection. Near Bypass Road.

🛒 Lotus's / Tops Market

Main supermarkets with imported products, fresh produce and household goods. Multiple locations throughout Hua Hin. Good for daily shopping.

🌙 Night Markets

Hua Hin Night Market (daily) and Cicada Market (weekends) offer fresh food, clothing, crafts and street food. Essential part of expat social life.

🥩 Weekend Market (Talat Jatujak)

Saturday and Sunday market near Soi 72 with fresh produce, meat, fish, Thai street food and household items. Very local and affordable.

🍷 Villa Market / Tops

Premium imported food stores with European products, international cheeses, wines and specialty items. Higher prices but best selection of western goods.

📦 Online Shopping

Lazada and Shopee deliver to Hua Hin within 1–3 days. Kerry Express and J&T are the main couriers. Most electronics and household items available online.

🎓 Schools & Education

Hua Hin has several international and bilingual schools suitable for expat children.

🏫 Hua Hin International School

British curriculum (Cambridge). Ages 3–18. Small classes, individual attention. Recognised by the UK, Australian and US universities. Most popular with European expats.

🏫 Sunflower Kindergarten

Well-regarded English-language kindergarten near the town centre. Popular with expat families with young children.

🏫 Thai Bilingual Schools

Several government and private Thai schools offer bilingual programs. Good option for families staying long-term who want children to integrate into Thai society.

Note: Waiting lists can be long at international schools. Apply at least 6 months before your planned start date. Tuition ranges from ฿150,000–฿350,000 per year depending on the school and year group.

🤝 Expat Social Life

Hua Hin has a thriving expat community of around 15,000 foreigners from 30+ countries. It's easy to make friends and find your tribe.

⛳ Golf

Hua Hin has 9 golf courses within easy driving distance. Many expats join golf societies that play 3–4 times per week. A great way to meet people on arrival.

🍺 Expat Bars & Restaurants

Soi Bintabaht and the Night Market area have dozens of expat-friendly bars. Regular quiz nights, live music and sports screenings throughout the week.

🏃 Hua Hin Hash House Harriers

Weekly running/walking social club. Extremely inclusive and social. One of the best ways to meet long-term expats and explore areas you'd never find alone.

🎭 Hua Hin Music Festival

Annual event (usually January/February) near Khao Takiab beach. Free entry, multiple stages, major Thai and international acts. A highlight of the expat calendar.

💬 Facebook Groups

"Expats in Hua Hin" and "Hua Hin Expats" Facebook groups are essential for local tips, recommendations, buying/selling, and social events. Over 25,000 members combined.

🏊 Water Sports & Fitness

Kitesurfing, paddleboarding, cycling groups, yoga studios and fitness centres. Hua Hin has an active outdoor community — easy to stay healthy here.

🗣️ Language

English is widely spoken in Hua Hin's expat zones, but learning basic Thai makes life much easier and locals love the effort.

📚 Thai Language Schools

Several language schools in Hua Hin offer group and private Thai lessons. Walen Language School and AUA are well-regarded. Budget ฿300–฿600/hour for private lessons.

📱 Useful Apps

Google Translate with camera mode is essential for menus and signs. Duolingo has a Thai course for basics. ThaiPod101 is popular for structured learning.

💬 Essential Thai Phrases

"Sabai dee mai?" (How are you?), "Phet nit noi" (A little spicy), "Tao rai?" (How much?), "Kop khun krap/ka" (Thank you). Even basic Thai gets smiles and better prices.

🔒 Safety & Security

Hua Hin is considered one of the safest places in Thailand for expats. Violent crime against foreigners is extremely rare.

🚔 General Safety

Hua Hin is very safe for daily life. Normal precautions apply — don't flash valuables, watch your bag in crowded markets, and be careful on motorbikes (most expat accidents happen here).

🏍️ Road Safety

Traffic is the biggest safety concern. Thailand has high road accident rates. Wear a helmet on motorbikes — non-negotiable. Get a valid licence before renting a vehicle.

🏥 Emergency Numbers

Police: 191 | Ambulance: 1669 | Tourist Police: 1155 (English-speaking) | Bangkok Hospital Hua Hin: 032-616-800. Save these in your phone on arrival.

💊 Health Precautions

Stay hydrated in the heat, use high SPF sunscreen, drink only bottled or filtered water. Dengue fever exists — use mosquito repellent. Get hepatitis A & B vaccinations before arrival.
